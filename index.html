I was looking at spec sheets of my crappy monitor one of my dad's colleagues donated to me so see what would decrease its latency. WHY YOUR PC DOES NOT MATTER (for sweaty games)

It might be surprising to see a PC be second to the monitor, but that's because I think that for most games, a good enough pc (~$800) is the exact same as a crazy pc (~5000). Being sweaty is all about bottlenecks. As long as something is not a bottleneck, it doesn't need to be upgraded. I think a lot of people at this point know that having a good pc is not a substitute for skill, as evidenced by the jokes like "he has a better gaming chair". I want to reiterate this point so people (prob parents) don't waste their money. Just looking online and seeing my friends PC's, the people who care about their pc do not play sweaty games, and people who sweat in games could not care less about their pc. Most pros in apex are still running 10 year old graphic cards because 10 year old graphic cards can run apex at 200+ fps. This is totally different then in rpg games like cyberpunk or TLOU where the PC is the bottleneck. So again, as long as you do a little research (or even chatgpt it) you can probably get a pc that can run your game at 200fps for relatively cheap. *With the ram shortage now this might be a little harder but 16gigs of ddr4 is still only $70 more expensive than it was a year ago.

Why your monitor matters

Your monitor is literally the only thing you see about your game. That is all you have to base your mouse and keyboard movements off of. All of the effort your computer spent trying to render frames is wasted if your monitor is bad. My friends will upgrade their nice pc but then buy some random monitor and then complain about how their computer is trash. That has to stop. You can't talk about a setup without talking about how good your monitor is. By now people (who grind fps) have heard of things like input lag and refresh rate. A quick recap: input lag is basically the time it takes between when you monitor receives a signal, and when it actually starts to produce that signal on the screen. Refresh rate is how many times per second a monitor can "flash" an image on the screen. While these two things are very critical for being sweaty, monitors with very low input lag and high refresh rates have become cheap. These two things are dependant on the chip inside of the monitor, and chip technology has come a long way so having a powerful enough chip to have low input lag and high refresh rate is basically common to any monitor made within a decade. So it was only a mild surprise when I found a 200hz monitor for as low as 80 bucks on walmart. So then why are the 200hz monitors sold by benq and asus and sony over 300, 400 dollars? Because of something called pixel response time

What is pixel response time

Pixel response time is how long it takes for a pixel to change from one color to another. When something moves, pixels must quickly switch colors to properly display the new frame. If the pixels don't switch colors quickly enough, the colors become smeared and, in particularly fast motion or particularly slow pixels, this results in smeared,unreadable images. This is the slowest part of a monitor, and therefore is the most important part because it bottlenecks everything else.

Peripherals

Hot take, I think your mousepad matters as much as the mouse (at least for tracking fast targets). At least this was my experince playing when upgrading from a mousepad my dad got from a confrence to an acutal mousepad. If you really think about it, the total user end to end latency (from mouse movement to screen update) is on the order of ms, but the time it takes for my to physcially move my mouse and track something is probaly on the order of 100s of ms, and is super dependant on the friction of the mouse, so it kinda makes sense that since a good mousepad can reduce the static friction (the initial friction that keeps the mouse from moving) of the mouse can reduce how long it takes from when you deicde to move the mouse to when the pixels on the screen start to change. As for the mouse itself, polling rate (how fast the mouse sends signals to the computer) is important as well as how fast the sensor in the mouse can detect movement. In 2026 most mice that are marketed as gaming mice generally have good sensors and high polling rates for around 20 bucks.
